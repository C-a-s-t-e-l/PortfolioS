Featured Paper

Anxiety Levels and Academic Performance Among STEM Students at Iloilo City National High School


This quantitative research investigates the relationship between anxiety levels and academic performance among STEM students at Iloilo City National High School. Utilizing a correlational design, the study assessed 67 randomly selected STEM students during the school year 2023-2024. Data collection involved the State-Trait Anxiety Inventory (STAI) for anxiety levels and the students' grade point averages (GPA) for academic performance. The findings highlighted severe levels of anxiety among students but revealed no significant correlation between anxiety levels and academic performance. This research provides insights for educational stakeholders to address anxiety and foster academic success.


Technologies used:
State-Trait Anxiety Inventory (STAI): For measuring anxiety levels.
Statistical Package for Social Sciences (SPSS): For data analysis (T-tests, ANOVA, etc.)
Google Forms: For data collection.

The Effect Of Welcome Plant (Zamioculcas Sp.) Leaves Extract And Diatomaceous Earth On Mortality Time Of Cockroaches (Blattodea Sp.)


This quantitative true-experimental study aimed to determine the Effect of Welcome plant leaves extract against Cockroaches and its potential as an alternative to commercial pesticides given different proportions of treatments. The study was conducted last March 21, 2024, at Colegio de San Jose. This quantitative inquiry focuses only on the mortality time of Cockroaches given different treatments of Welcome Plant Leaf extract.


Technologies used:
Statistical Package for Social Sciences (SPSS): For statistical analysis
The One-way Analysis of Variance (ANOVA):To analyze cockroach mortality time differences using t-test, ANOVA, and Tukey HSD.
Ms Excel: For data collection

Intelligence Dominance Grounded On Gardner's Multiple Intelligence Theory Among Grade 11 Senior High School Students


This study explores the application of Howard Gardner's Multiple Intelligences Theory among Grade 11 Senior High School students at Iloilo City National High School. It identifies the dominant and weakest intelligences across different academic strands, including ABM, STEM, GAS, HUMSS, and TVL. Using a descriptive research design, the study collected and analyzed data to provide insights into intelligence patterns and their implications for personalized learning strategies.


Technologies used:
Research-Made Test (RMT): Custom questionnaires designed based on Gardner's theory.
Google Forms: For efficient data collection.
Statistical Package for Social Sciences (SPSS): For descriptive analysis
